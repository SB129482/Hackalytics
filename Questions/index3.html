Interjection Exercises

Individuals who have interjection issues frequently use filler words. Practice the following sentences without using the indicated fillers such as "um" or "uh" or "you know."

  • Um, well you see, the thing is um, a bit complex.
  • Oh, well um, let me think about it about before I get back to you.
  • Well, um, let's see, I guess, um, we could, like, go to the, uh, park and have a, you know, picnic or whatever.
  • Hmm, well, I'm not, like, entirely sure, but, um, I think, uh, we're supposed to, you know, meet them at, uh, seven o'clock.
  • So, um, yeah, I mean, I think we should, like, probably, you know, finish this, uh, project by, um, Friday, if that's okay with everyone?
  • Um, well, I heard that, like, there's, um, going to be, uh, a really cool, you know, concert downtown, so maybe we should, like, check it out?
  • Hmm, let me, uh, think about it for a, um, second... Oh, yeah, I remember now! We, um, need to, you know, buy some groceries before the store closes.
  • Well, um, I guess we could, like, um, watch a, uh, movie tonight, or maybe, you know, just stay in and, uh, relax.
  • Um, so, like, I was thinking, maybe we could, you know, go grab some, uh, pizza or something for dinner tonight?
  • Um, well, you know, I was thinking that, uh, maybe we could, like, um, go to that, uh, new coffee shop, grab a, um, latte or something, and, uh, just chill for a bit, you know?
